Tradicional dishes

Introduction

Japanese dishes, each offering unique flavors, textures, and cultural significance.

Gyoza

Japanese dumplings filled with minced pork, garlic, and cabbage, usually pan-fried to have a crispy bottom and soft top.

Onigiri

Rice balls often shaped by hand and wrapped in seaweed, filled with ingredients like salted salmon, pickled plum, or tuna mayo.

Ramen

A noodle soup dish with a rich broth (soy, miso, or pork-based), topped with ingredients like sliced pork, boiled eggs, and green onions.

Tempura

Lightly battered and deep-fried vegetables or seafood, served crispy with a dipping sauce or salt.

Sushi

Vinegared rice paired with raw or cooked fish, vegetables, or egg, served in various forms like nigiri or rolls.

Tonkatsu

Breaded and deep-fried pork cutlet, typically served with shredded cabbage and a tangy, savory sauce.
